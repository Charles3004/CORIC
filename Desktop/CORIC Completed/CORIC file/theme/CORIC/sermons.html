"If Ye Love Me, Keep My Commandments"

by Pst. Oloche Adaji

John 14:15 (KJV) – "If ye love me, keep my commandments."

Introduction: Love is not just an emotion—it is a commitment that reveals itself through obedience. Jesus gives us a measuring line for true love toward Him: obedience. The Christian walk is not just about proclaiming love with words, but proving it with actions.

I. Understanding the Context: Jesus, during His farewell discourse, prepares His disciples for His departure. Amid upcoming trials, He stresses that true love is shown through obedience, not emotion.

II. What Does It Mean to "Keep My Commandments"?
– To treasure and obey (Psalm 119:11).
– Not selective obedience (James 2:10).
– Obeying with the right heart—not out of fear, but love.

III. Love and Obedience: Inseparable Partners
– John 15:10 shows they go hand-in-hand.
– Love without obedience = empty confession.
– Obedience without love = cold legalism.

IV. Examples from Scripture:
– Abraham’s obedience (Genesis 22).
– Saul’s disobedience (1 Samuel 15).
– Jesus’ perfect example (Philippians 2:8).

V. Why Do Believers Struggle to Obey?
– Lack of intimacy with Christ.
– Carnal desires war against the spirit (Gal. 5:17).
– The world downplays holiness.

"Marital Dignity: Restoring Honor to the Covenant of Marriage"

by Pst. (Mrs.) Becky Oloche Adaji

Hebrews 13:4 (KJV) – “Marriage is honourable in all, and the bed undefiled: but whoremongers and adulterers God will judge.”

Also Reading: Ephesians 5:25-33

Introduction: In a world where marriage is often reduced to convenience, trends, or status, the dignity of the marital covenant is under attack. God's design for marriage is sacred and honorable. When honored, families thrive and God is glorified.

1. What is Marital Dignity?
– Dignity means nobility, worth, and honor.
– Marital dignity means recognizing God’s glory in the institution and treating the union with deep respect and integrity.
– It’s not just being married—it’s how you’re married.

2. Why is Marriage Honorable?
– Instituted by God: Genesis 2:18–24 – Marriage is God’s idea.
– Reflects Christ and the Church: Ephesians 5:32 – A divine mystery of partnership and love.
– Tool for Dominion and Legacy: Malachi 2:15 – God seeks godly offspring through honorable marriages.

Conclusion: Restoring honor to marriage means restoring God’s place in the home. Let us uphold the dignity of marriage with love, integrity, and reverence for God’s design.

"The Power That Changes Everything"

by Pst. Oloche Adaji

Acts 10:38 (KJV) – “How God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Ghost and with power: who went about doing good, and healing all that were oppressed of the devil; for God was with him.”

Introduction: True transformation doesn't come from human effort—it comes from the power of God. This same power that anointed Jesus is still at work today, changing lives and breaking oppression.

1. The Source of Power:
– God is the source of true power.
– It’s divine, not man-made.

2. The Substance of Power:
– Jesus was anointed with the Holy Ghost and with power.
– The Holy Spirit brings visible, tangible results.

3. The Purpose of Power:
– To do good and destroy oppression.
– Power is for impact, not just status.

4. The Presence Behind the Power:
– “For God was with Him.”
– Divine presence fuels divine results.

Conclusion: The same power that was upon Jesus is available to us today. If we want to see real change in our lives, families, and nation—we must seek His power and presence.
